Summer 2023 Work Term Report

Introduction

Welcome to my first work term report! In this report, I will go over the first 4 month term out of my two 8 month co-op term (May to Sept 2020) at Nissan Canada Inc, what I accomplished, the skills I developed, key takeaways.

About Nissan

Established in 1933, Nissan is renowned multinational automotive manufacturer and stands as a global leader in the automotive industry, known for its innovation, quality, and commitment to sustainability. Nissan Canada Inc. is headquartered in Mississauga, ON, Canada with over 400 employees while the main Nissan Motor Corporation is headquartered in Yokohama, Japan. Nissan's models cater to diverse markets with an extensive range of vehicles, including electric cars, SUVs, trucks, and commercial vehicles. INFINITI is Nissan's luxury vehicle division known for sophisticated design and comfort.

Vehicle Connected Services is a rapidly growing field within the automotive industry that leverages computer science and technology to provide innovative and enhanced experiences for vehicle owners. Since modern vehicles are equipped with sophisticated infotainment and connectivity systems, creating user-friendly interfaces, integrating smartphone connectivity, and ensuring seamless communication between vehicles and external systems is crucial.
Joining the team at Nissan has been a remarkable chapter in my co-op journey. As a global leader in the automotive industry, Nissan's reputation for innovation, quality, and commitment to sustainability with their lineup of EV's has been evident from day one.

Job Description

As a Vehicle Connected Services Co-op at Nissan Canada Inc., my role was centered around actively participating in the development and testing of mobile apps and web portals. Working closely with a dedicated team, I had the exciting opportunity to contribute to the creation of innovative solutions that enhance the connectivity and experience of Nissan and INFINITI vehicle owners. My top responsibilities included collaborating on app features, engaging in testing procedures to ensure app functionality and improving current VCS processes using automation.

One of the unique aspects of my job was the opportunity to be hands-on with the vehicles themselves and directly engage in testing the functionality of in-vehicle features and mobile apps. This experience allowed me to bridge the gap between software development and real-world automotive technology. My SQL skills were important, as it enabled me to interact with databases, extract valuable insights, and ensure efficient data management. Proficiency in utilizing JIRA was essential for reporting issues, tracking progress of bug fixes, and validating them. Collaboration was also key, as I actively participated in cross-functional teams, collaborating with developers, designers, and marketing teams to achieve our shared objectives. My ability to contribute effectively in team settings and communicate technical concepts clearly was pivotal in driving project success. Although I have learned these skills in class and from previous experiences, this role enabled me to leverage these for real-world applications. This combination of these skills was important in not only fulfilling the requirements of the role but also in (literally) driving innovation for Vehicle Connected Services at Nissan Canada.

With four months of valuable experience behind me and an exciting four-month journey still ahead, I am grateful for the opportunities and lessons learned during this co-op, and I am enthusiastic about the positive impact these experiences will have on this second term as well as my future career within the automotive industry.
